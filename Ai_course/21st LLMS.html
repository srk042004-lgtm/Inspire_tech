Semantic Analysis

Class 66 & 67: Text Classification & Sentiment Analysis

Text Classification is the foundational task of assigning categories to raw text based on its content. In these classes, we transition from simple keyword matching to Semantic Classification using supervised learning. You will learn to build a Sentiment Analysis engine that can distinguish between "Positive," "Negative," and "Neutral" emotions in social media posts or customer reviews. We explore the TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) algorithm to weight the importance of words across a corpus. You will also implement the Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Multinomial Naive Bayes classifiers to handle high-dimensional text data. At Inspire Tech, we demonstrate how businesses use this to monitor brand reputation automatically. This class provides the metrics—Precision, Recall, and F1-Score—needed to validate how well your AI truly "understands" the intent behind human writing.

Contextual Dialogue

Class 68: Advanced Contextual AI Chatbots

Advanced chatbots move beyond single-turn responses to maintain a cohesive conversation over multiple exchanges. In this class, we implement Dialogue Management systems that use State Tracking to remember user preferences throughout a session. You will learn to integrate external APIs, allowing your chatbot to pull real-time data like weather, stock prices, or flight status to answer user queries. We cover Entity Extraction using pre-trained models like Spacy’s NER (Named Entity Recognition) to identify dates, locations, and names in a sentence. You will also explore Slot Filling, a technique where the bot asks follow-up questions until it has all the necessary info to complete a task. At our Nowshera lab, we build a "concierge bot" capable of handling complex booking workflows, proving that your AI can be both conversational and highly functional.

Generative Era

Class 69: ChatGPT & The Transformer Revolution

This class introduces the Transformer Architecture, the breakthrough that made ChatGPT possible. We move away from sequential processing (RNNs) to Self-Attention Mechanisms, which allow the model to look at every word in a sentence simultaneously to understand context. You will learn the concept of Large Language Models (LLMs)—models trained on trillions of words to predict the next token in a sequence. We explore the GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) family and how "Pre-training" on massive data followed by "Fine-tuning" creates a versatile AI. At Inspire Tech, we discuss the "Zero-shot" and "Few-shot" learning capabilities of these models, where they can perform tasks they weren't explicitly trained for just by following instructions. This class marks the shift from building specialized tools to utilizing general-purpose artificial intelligence that can write code, poetry, and complex analysis.

Capstone Project

Class 70: NLP Final Project - Personal AI Assistant

For your NLP graduation project, you will build a Custom AI Knowledge Assistant. This project uses RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), a technique that connects a Large Language Model to your own private documents or local data. You will learn to use Vector Databases (like ChromaDB or Pinecone) to store document "embeddings" for fast semantic search. Your system will take a user's question, find the relevant information from your custom data, and use an LLM to generate a natural, accurate response. This project solves the "hallucination" problem of AI by grounding it in facts. By the end of this class, you will have a functional application that can act as a tutor, a legal assistant, or a customer support expert. This project is the ultimate demonstration of your ability to combine traditional NLP pre-processing with modern Generative AI power.
